    Consumer Rights

    Consumer rights are a set of legal entitlements and protections provided to individuals to ensure fair treatment in the marketplace. These rights are designed to safeguard consumers against exploitation, ensure access to safe products and services, and empower them to make informed choices. Below is a description of key consumer rights: Right to Safety Protects consumers from hazardous goods and services that may harm their health or safety. Right to Information Ensures consumers have access to accurate and adequate information about products and services to make informed decisions. Right to Choose Guarantees the freedom to select from a variety of goods and services at competitive prices without being coerced. Right to be Heard Assures consumers that their grievances and feedback will be considered in policy-making and product improvement.

    Features

    Following Are The Features of Consumer Rights

    • Protection
    • Awareness
    • Redressal
    • Education
    • Safety
    • Quality

    Case Study

    The debate around the high sugar content in Mondelez India-owned 'Health' drink Bournvita is still on days after a social media influencer called out the product in a viral video. A social media storm arose when Instagram influencer Revant Himatsingka had to take down his video after receiving a legal notice from Cadbury. Here's what has happened so far: Revant Himatsingka claimed that one of the ingredients in Bournvita known as 'Colour (150C)' can cause cancer and reduce immunity. He also said that on Bournvita's ingredient label, they have 50 grams of sugar per 100 grams. "I will request parents to please stop getting their kids addicted to these products. I request the government to take action against these false claims," the influencer said.

    Why "Tayari Diabetes Ki" ??

    In his now-deleted video posted on April 1, Revant Himatsingka called out Bournvita for packaging their product as a 'health' drink. "The tagline of Bournvita is ‘taiyari jeet ki’ but rather, it should be ‘taiyari diabetes ki’,” he said in the video, adding that Cadbury is not a health company but a chocolate company.

    Conclusion

    In conclusion, the six consumer rights-Right to Consumer Education, Right to Choose, Right to Safety, Right to Seek Compensation, Right to Be Heard, and Right to Be Informed-are essential for empowering consumers and protecting them from unfair practices. These rights ensure that consumers are well-informed, can make independent choices, and are safeguarded against exploitation or harm. By spreading awareness about these rights, we can create a fair marketplace where consumers can confidently hold businesses accountable and make decisions that benefit them and society as a whole.
